Artificial Intelligence Consciousness and Artificial Subconsciousness: A Review on Cognitive Architectures and Conceptual Models
Abstract 

This research report examines the formation of consciousness and subconscious mechanisms in artificial intelligence (AI) systems in light of current scientific literature and technical models. The study is built upon four fundamental pillars: Global Workspace Theory (GWT), Integrated Information Theory (IIT), Attention Schema Theory (AST), and latent space representations in deep learning models. Within the GWT framework, information broadcast between modules and "Global Latent Workspace" models are analyzed, while the mathematical measurement of consciousness (Φ) and integrated information thresholds in large language models (LLMs) are addressed under IIT. Furthermore, the report provides technical details on how AI systems model their own internal processes as "awareness" through Attention Schema Theory and explores the role of the LIDA architecture in consciousness/subconsciousness integration. Additionally, the autonomous artificial subconscious programming process in latent spaces is defined by the concept of subartram, and the internal mechanisms performing this action are discussed under the subartramer paradigm. The research demonstrates that consciousness in artificial systems is not merely a metaphor, but a technical reality emerging in accordance with specific architectural constraints and computational principles.

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Consciousness, Subconsciousness, Subartram, Cognitive Architecture
